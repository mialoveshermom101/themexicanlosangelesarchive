Project Notes

This archive is dedicated to my father and to the young Mexican-American children in Los Angeles who, like me, were never handed a full picture of themselves. My father was born in Mexico and came to L.A. chasing the dream we all recognize; when he died, the clearest doorway to my Mexican-ness went with him. Later, spiraling through the Internet Archive, I started finding these early community-made manuals and curricula from the ’80s and ’90s — things teachers and organizers put together specifically for Mexican-American kids. I had never seen this in middle school or high school. It made me wonder why nothing like that exists now, in a time when we live online, and what it would look like to translate that care into a digital, searchable, map-based form. So I started building it myself.

What you’re seeing here is an early map — a first pass at gathering the sites, stories, and traces of Mexican and Chicanx life in L.A., the parts that get built, erased, and fought for. It will grow as I learn more. If you know of places, people, or histories that should live here too, please tell me at miaarambula321@gmail.com

About Mexican Los Angeles Archive

Mexican Los Angeles Archive is a comprehensive digital collection documenting the Mexican-American experience in Los Angeles County from 1771 to the present day.

Research Methodology

This archive combines historical records, oral histories, maps, and multimedia materials to create an interconnected narrative of Mexican Los Angeles. Materials are sourced from libraries, archives, community organizations, and personal collections.
